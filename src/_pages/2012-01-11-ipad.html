Readability helps zap online clutter and saves web articles in a comfortable reading view. No matter where you are or what device you use, your reading will be there.

Readability Everywhere

To get the most of Readability you can add it to all your devices and computers. That way, you'll be able to access or add to your reading list from anywhere. It's available for iPhone, iPad, mobile Web, your favorite Web browser, Kindle, Twitter clients, RSS readers and more.  Here’s a complete list of ways to use Readability

First Launch

When you first launch the Readability app it will download all of the articles you've saved for later. Once the articles are downloaded you can read them whenever you want, even offline. This can take some time depending on how many articles you have saved. You can see the progress in the loading bar at the top.

Your Reading List

The default view to Readability is the Reading List. This is where all the articles you’ve saved are stored. You can start reading right away by simply tapping on an article. You can view your Favorites and Archives Lists by tapping on the Reading List item in the menu bar.

Syncing Readability

You can manually sync your lists to make sure you have all your articles available by pulling down on your list or by tapping the reload button in the menu bar.

Searching

Looking for something specific? Tap the search icon and you can search all of articles in your lists instantly, even if they’re archived.

Managing your articles

There are a number of ways to manage what articles appear in your lists. The first is managing them one at a time. To archive, favorite or delete an article from your list you can swipe to the right.

You can also archive and delete multiple articles at once by tapping ‘edit’ in the menu bar. Then simply select the articles you want to move and chose the appropriate option at the bottom of the screen.

The in-app browser

Readability includes a web browser that let’s you surf the Web and instantly convert any page into a comfortable reading view. The access it tap the ‘+’ icon in the menu bar. You can also add any page to your Reading list by selecting ‘Read Later’.

The Reading View

Welcome to distraction free reading. The reading view let's you scroll through your saved articles. Swipe to the right to go back to your Reading List.

You can favorite, archive, delete or share the article tapping on the screen and choosing the option you're looking for.

At the top of every article there’s a button that will display the article in the original Web view. You can also tap on the URL.

Customizing Readability

You can change the font, size, color and column width through the style menu. This makes it easy for you to customize a reading view that suits your needs. Swipe the fonts left or right to change them. Note: While font and color can be changed in the Settings Panel in the main menu bar, font size and column width settings are only accessible from the reading view as shown below.

Share what you’re reading

You can connect your Twitter and Facebook accounts to Readability and share what you’re reading. Tap the bottom right icon on an article view (you may need to tap the screen to bring up the menu) and you’ll see all the various ways to share it. If you select the twitter or Facebook icons you’ll be asked to login (you only need to do this once). From then on you’ll be able to Tweet or post to Facebook. Other actions include; Emailing a link to the article, copying a link to the article or opening in Safari.
